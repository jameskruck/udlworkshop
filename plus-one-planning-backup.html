UDL Plus One Workshop

Plus One Planning

The Plus One Strategy, introduced by Thomas J. Tobin and Kirsten T. Behling, is a practical approach to Universal Design for Learning (UDL). Instead of overhauling an entire course, educators add just one new option to improve accessibility and flexibility.

In this activity, you are going to start your own UDL-enhancement process by identifying one simple improvement you could make. For example, you might reduce barriers for learners by:

  • Adding a short video explanation alongside a written guide
  • Creating an interactive simulation to complement static diagrams>
  • Allowing learners to submit a recorded response instead of writing an essay

Part 3: Identify Relevant UDL Principles

You've identified a pinch point and reflected on the manner in which you teach this topic. The next step is to consider how you could improve the delivery of this topic by using UDL principles. Remember, for this activity we are focusing on one method of improvement. After today, you will be implementing UDL as an iterative task, so even if you identify more than one method, just choose one for now.

Part 4: Plan Your Plus-One Enhancement

Examples to Offer:

  • Math: Create short video demonstrations with step-by-step annotations
  • History: Add audio readings of documents with modern language translations
  • Biology: Include interactive simulations alongside static diagrams
