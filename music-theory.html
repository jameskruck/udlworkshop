Modal Interchange: Definition

Modal interchange is a compositional technique where chords are borrowed from parallel modes to add harmonic variety to a piece of music. For example, a piece in C major might borrow chords from C minor.

Technical Details

In traditional harmony, compositions typically use chords from a single mode. Modal interchange expands these options by allowing borrowed chords from parallel modes. Common examples include:

  • The flat sixth chord (♭VI) borrowed from minor mode
  • The diminished second chord (ii°) from minor mode
  • The flat third chord (♭III) from minor mode

These borrowed chords create tension and emotional depth in the harmony.
