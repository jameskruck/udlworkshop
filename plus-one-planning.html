Planning Your Plus One Enhancement

You've experienced UDL principles in action and learned about different ways to make learning more accessible. Now it's time to plan a specific enhancement for your own teaching.

Remember:

  • Start small - choose one specific area to enhance
  • Focus on your biggest "pain point" first
  • Plan for something you can implement soon

Step 1: Identify Your Pinch Point

A "pinch point" is a specific part of your course where:

  • Students consistently struggle
  • You receive frequent questions or requests for clarification
  • Assessment results show lower understanding
  • You notice decreased engagement

Step 2: Analyze Your Current Approach

Consider how you currently teach this content:

  • What methods do you use?
  • What materials do students access?
  • How do students practice or apply the content?
  • How do students demonstrate their understanding?

Step 3: Apply UDL Principles

Engagement

How can you spark interest and maintain motivation?

Representation

How can you present the information in different ways?

Action & Expression

How can students demonstrate their understanding?

Step 4: Choose Your Plus One Enhancement

Choose ONE enhancement that:

  • You can implement relatively quickly
  • Will make a meaningful difference
  • Fits your available resources
  • You feel confident about trying

Example Enhancements by UDL Principle:

Engagement

  • Add real-world examples and applications
  • Include student choice in topics or methods
  • Create collaborative learning opportunities

Representation

  • Create step-by-step video demonstrations
  • Add interactive visualizations
  • Provide annotated examples

Action & Expression

  • Offer multiple assessment formats
  • Allow for peer teaching/explanation
  • Enable digital or physical submissions
