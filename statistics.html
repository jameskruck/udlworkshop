Understanding the Chi-Square Test

The chi-square test of independence examines whether two categorical variables are independent of each other. This test is fundamental in statistical analysis for determining relationships between categorical data.

Technical Details

The test compares observed frequencies with expected frequencies calculated under the assumption of independence. The test statistic is calculated as:

Χ² = Σ((O-E)²/E)

Where:

  • O represents observed frequencies
  • E represents expected frequencies
  • Degrees of freedom = (r-1)(c-1)
  • r is the number of rows
  • c is the number of columns

The null hypothesis states that variables are independent; the alternative hypothesis states they are related.
